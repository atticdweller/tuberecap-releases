# TubeRecap

**Turn any YouTube video into a written summary — 100% on your own computer.**

Paste a YouTube link (or just copy one — TubeRecap will notice) and it
listens to the video, writes down everything said, and gives you a clean,
organized summary. Nothing you summarize ever leaves your machine.

## Install

Go to **[the latest release](https://github.com/atticdweller/tuberecap-releases/releases/latest)** and download:

| Your computer | Download | Then |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Windows** | `TubeRecap-Setup-….exe` | Double-click it. If Windows shows a blue "protected your PC" screen, click **More info → Run anyway**. |
| **Mac** (Apple Silicon) | `TubeRecap-….dmg` | Open it, drag TubeRecap to Applications. The first time: **right-click the app → Open → Open**. |

That's it — no other steps, no accounts, nothing else to install.

The first time you open TubeRecap it downloads its AI brain (about 7 GB —
give it a few minutes on good Wi-Fi; it resumes automatically if
interrupted). After that it's ready instantly, works offline for browsing
your library, and keeps itself up to date on Windows automatically.

## Using it

1. Copy any YouTube link, anywhere. A notification pops up: *"Summarize this video?"* — click it.
2. Or open TubeRecap and paste the link in the big box.
3. A few minutes later (depending on video length): a full summary, saved
   forever in your Library. Click any past video to re-read its summary or
   the full transcript.
